Journey to explore Jaipur

"The Pink City"
Jaipur is the capital of the royal state of Rajasthan. Along with Delhi and Agra, Jaipur forms the Golden Triangle and hails as one of the most famous tourist circuits in the country. Rajputs ruled Jaipur for many centuries and developed as a planned city in the 17th century AD.

Popular destination

One of the largest cities in India, Jaipur is also home to all the modern amenities with some of the most exotic hotels and resorts in the world. The city boasts an international airport and is also very well connected by rail and road. This makes Jaipur the gateway to Rajasthan - the starting point for places to visit in the interiors of the state.

  • Jaipur

    Amer Fort

    Cradled on the top of a hill near Jaipur lies the Amer Fort, one of the most magnificent palaces in India.

  • Jaipur

    Hawa Mahal

    The massive edifice of Hawa Mahal stands at the intersection of the main road in Jaipur, Badi Chaupad and was built by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh in the year 1799.

  • Jaipur

    Jantar Mantar

    Located near the City Palace in the regal city of Jaipur, Jantar Mantar is the largest stone astronomical observatory in the world.

  • Jaipur

    Birla Temple

    The magnificent Birla Mandir in Jaipur is a Hindu temple that forms a part of one of the several Birla temples located all around the country.

  • Jaipur

    Albert Hall Museum

    Situated in the Ram Niwas Garden of Jaipur, the Albert Hall Museum is the oldest museum in Rajasthan.

  • Jaipur

    Jaigarh Fort

    Jaigarh Fort is a grand structure perched on the top of the 'Cheel ka Teela' hills in the Pink City of Jaipur.
