Journey to explore Mumbai

"The City of Dreams"
Mumbai, the capital city of the Indian state of Maharashtra, is a spectacular paradox of chaos and hope, glamour and squalor, modernity and tradition. Famously known as the City of Dreams, Mumbai – formerly known as Bombay - Mumbai is a beautifully blended melting pot of cultures and lifestyles.

Popular destination

The Gateway of India in Colaba is perhaps one of Mumbai’s most prominent monuments, established in 1924 to welcome King George V and Queen Mary. And situated not too far from it is the renowned Taj Mahal Palace. When you travel to Mumbai, pay a visit to the Elephanta Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site situated on a small island about 11km from the city, accessible via a ferry. Given that Mumbai lies along the shore, its beaches like Juhu Beach and Chowpatty are great to relax. Chowpatty Beach is on one end of Marine Drive, an arc-shaped road along the coast where you can enjoy a walk in the evening.

    Film City Mumbai

    Spread across a vast area of 520 acres, the Film City is comprised of about twenty indoor studios and is located in Aarey Colony, Mumbai, Film City is often regarded as a home of Bollywood.

    Hanging Gardens

    Hanging Garden is a terrace garden situated on top of the Malabar Hill, right next to the Kamla Nehru Park.
